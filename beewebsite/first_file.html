Why Bumblebees are Awesome

About Bumblebees

Bumblebees are social insects with the genus Bombus. They are distinctive from honeybees is many ways. For example, their stingers do not become detached when they sting another animal. Another interesting fact about bumblebees is that they leave their scent on the flower evertime they visit a plant. This signals to other bumblebees that the nectar has already been taken by another bee.

There are many threats to the bumblebees. When it gets colder, the queen bumblebee will hibernate inside the soil. This makes them vulnerable to humans who may accidentally disturb the earth. Also, mowing the lawn make wild flowers less available to hungry bumblebees. This is why planting wild flowers are important to encourage bumblebees and other pollinators into the area. For example, dandelions can be left to grow.

Examples of Bumblebees

  • UK: Buff-tailed bumblebee (Bombus terrestris)
  • UK: White-tailed bumblebee (Bombus lucorum)
  • UK: Tree bumblebee (Bombus hypnorum)
  • UK: Garden bumblebee (Bombus hortorum)
  • UK: Red-tailed bumblebee (Bombus lapidarius)
  • UK: Common carder bee (Bombus pascuorum)
